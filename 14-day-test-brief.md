# 14-Day Test Brief

Use this template to turn any finding from today's exercises into a structured test you can run this week.

---

## The One Rule

One hypothesis. One change. 14 days. One metric.

If you write down more than one change, you won't know which one worked.

---

## Your Hypothesis

Fill in the blanks:

> **"If we [change one specific thing], then [specific audience] will [do this measurable thing], and we will see it in 14 days."**

**Example — from the donor export exercise:**
> "If we send a two-email re-engagement sequence to the 42 P2P donors who have never received a second-gift ask, then at least 10 will open and 2 will donate, measurable by [date 14 days from now]."

**Example — from the donation audit:**
> "If we add Apple Pay and Google Pay to our donation form, then mobile visitors will complete donations at a higher rate, measurable by comparing mobile conversion rate week-over-week in GA4."

**Your hypothesis:**
> "If we __________, then __________ will __________, measurable by __________."

## Napkin Math (optional but useful)

Before you run it, estimate the upside — not to forecast, but to decide if it's worth doing.

| | Low end | Likely | High end |
|-|---------|--------|----------|
| Audience size | | | |
| Response / conversion rate | | | |
| Average gift or action value | | | |
| **Estimated impact** | | | |

> If even the low-end estimate is worth your time, run the test.  
> If the high-end estimate isn't exciting, find a different hypothesis.

---

## After 14 Days

Answer these three questions:

1. **What happened?** *(the number, not the narrative)*

2. **What did we learn?** *(one sentence — even a flat result is a result)*

3. **What is the next question?** *(what would you test next based on this?)*

---

## Three Tests Worth Running This Week

Based on the most common findings from the workshop exercises — pick the one that fits your situation:

**Test A — Donor export (P2P / lapsed segment)**
> "If we send [segment name] a re-engagement email referencing their original gift, then [X%] will open and [Y] will donate within 14 days."
- Pull the segment from your CRM using the revenue forensics prompt
- Send a plain-language email referencing their specific campaign or gift
- Measure opens, clicks, and completions

**Test B — Donation form friction**
> "If we reduce the required fields on our donation form from [X] to [Y], then our form completion rate will increase, measurable in GA4 over the next 14 days."
- Identify the highest-friction fields from your audit report
- Make one form change (remove a field, add autofill, remove email confirmation)
- Compare completion rate before and after

**Test C — High-intent page (GA4 finding)**
> "If we run £/$/500 of paid search against [high-CVR page] using [keyword set], then we will see [X] additional donations within 14 days."
- Identify your highest-converting organic page from GA4
- Build one small paid search campaign pointing to it
- Measure incremental donations vs. the control period
